What We Will Cover

The Introduction

The chapter emphasizes that Hermetic teachings have long been carefully preserved and shared only with those prepared to understand them. These principles, the authors argue, underlie many religious and philosophical systems worldwide and act as a common thread beneath their differences. Readers are encouraged to approach the material actively, applying the principles themselves rather than expecting simplified explanations. The chapter closes by stressing that understanding comes through readiness and reflection, not passive reading.

Chapter 1: The Hermetic Philosophy

This chapter traces the origins of Hermetic philosophy to ancient Egypt, presenting it as a foundational source of mystical and esoteric thought that has influenced cultures and religions across the world. Central to this tradition is Hermes Trismegistus, revered as the “Master of Masters,” whose teachings shaped astrology.

The Seven Principles

  1. Principle of Mentalism: reality is fundamentally mind like, and thoughts matter
  2. Principle of Correspondence: patterns repeat across levels, inner to outer, small to large
  3. Principle of Vibration: everything changes, and mind states can shift
  4. Principle of Polarity: opposites are two extremes of the same thing, fear and courage, hate and love
  5. Principle of Rhythm: life swings like a pendulum, and wisdom is learning to steady yourself
  6. Principle of Cause and Effect: nothing is random, and the goal is to become more cause than effect
  7. Principle of Gender: creative forces express in complementary modes
